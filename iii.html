Inês Castanheira
III

III is an audiovisual exploration of live coding, circuitbending, and glitch. Its soundscape is composed by a contrasting mixture of 8bit synthesized melodic sounds and distorted analog signals from circuit-bended artefacts, forming two distinct but overlapping layers that also bear deep contrasts in composition, from the digital patterned arrangements that emerge from the computer to the patternless analog signals from the circuitbended artefacts. Its image is generated in real time, consisting of sequences of visual patterns generated by an internal feedback loop that corrupts the original signal, which is directly influenced by the code that is used to generate the sound. Overall, this performance assumes itself as a piece that explores an abstract landscape with a certain dose of minimalism in sight, in which each performance returns alternate and, sometimes, unexpected outcomes.

Inês Castanheira, Mariana Sardon, Pedro Cardoso


Workshop Expanded Cinema: Live Coding to Live Cinema with Julio D'Escriván
FBAUP, Curtas Metragens CRL, ID+, i2ADS

" The present workshop proposes to develop an audiovisual performance with the group of participants, using live-coding approaches that will be introduced to participants in the first sessions and used in articulation with a range of systems and tools — depending also on the participants skills — for the production of moving images and sound. Participants will be organized in groups that will develop a multi-part performance during the six five sessions of the workshop, with two final presentations to take place in the evenings of May 15 and 16, at Cinema Passos Manuel (Porto) and Centro de Memória (Vila do Conde).
Participants will be introduced to Supercollider, Ixi-Lang and the Supercollider Pattern Library, starting from a low-level and conducting to the development of audio and midi/osc-control patterns that may be used in conjunction with video or VJ applications (as Resolume, VVVV, Max/Jitter, etc.) that can be controlled by live coding."
