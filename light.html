Inês Castanheira
Li#gH%t



Li#gH%t its a ongoing project that explores the hidden sounds and melodies present in the flashing light of electronic consumer devices, such as electric candles, toys and decoration lights. In the format of interactive installations the public is invited to discover the hidden sounds by manipulating them in games of light and sound using photovoltaic cell panels for playing and controlling the hidden sounds.
Over time, electric candles and other luminous devices have been collected for the project, using a mini pocket diy photovoltaic panel connected to a crystal earphone for listening the hidden sounds while searching on stores and fleamarkets.
The pulsation of the LEDs' light is carried out through the intermittent variation of the electrical signal generated by an integrated circuit. After experimenting with the electronic interior of some electric candles, it was discovered that most of them have sound circuits that generate the variation of light, thus imitating the flickering of the flame. One of the reasons for this may be related to the mass manufacture of sound integrated circuits with different applications in consumer electronic devices, reusing their sound component to create luminous effects on electronic devices.
To listen to the different sounds behind the light effect of the LED, a photovoltaic cell is used in connection with an amplifier or through the direct connection of electrical wires to a speaker. When approaching the cell close to the LED devices, this converts the light variation directly into electrical energy that can be converted into sound through an amplifier.
