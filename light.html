Inês Castanheira
Li#gH%t



Li#gH%t its a ongoing project that explores the hidden sounds and melodies present in the flashing light of electronic consumer devices, such as electric candles, toys and decoration lights. In the format of interactive installations the public is invited to discover the hidden sounds by manipulating them in games of light and sound using photovoltaic cell panels for playing and controlling the hidden sounds. Over time, electric candles and other luminous devices have been collected for the project, using a mini pocket diy photovoltaic panel connected to a crystal earphone for listening the hidden sounds while searching on stores and fleamarkets.
The pulsation of the LEDs' light is carried out through the intermittent variation of the electrical signal generated by an integrated circuit. After experimenting with the electronic interior of some electric candles, it was discovered that most of them have sound circuits that generate the variation of light, thus imitating the flickering of the flame. One of the reasons for this may be related to the mass manufacture of sound integrated circuits with different applications in consumer electronic devices, reusing their sound component to create luminous effects on electronic devices. To listen to the different sounds behind the light effect of the LED, a photovoltaic cell is used in connection with an amplifier. When approaching the cell close to the LED devices, the light variation is transformed into electrical energy, converting the light to sound through the amplifier.

Residency EXPAND! Art, Science and Tecnhology
at O Espaço do Tempo, Montemor-o-novo and Ciência Viva, Lisbon, 2018/2019
Presentation at Ciência Viva, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, Lisboa 2019



"EXPAND! Art, Science and Technology Residencies is a collaboration between O Espaço do Tempo, Ciência Viva and Audiência Zero. Together, we promote research projects in the digital arts with a strong technology research dimension. Over two weeks we will bring together creators at the intersection of art, science and technology to develop 11 prototypes of science mediation with a strong creative dimension. These contents will be presented to the public in two different moments: an exhibition at the Espaço do Tempo, in Montemor-o-Novo, organized with the local School Group; and an exhibition at the Pavilion of Knowledge – Ciência Viva Centre in Lisbon. The latter, scheduled for March 2019, will also include informal talks with artists in a relaxed sharing environment."
